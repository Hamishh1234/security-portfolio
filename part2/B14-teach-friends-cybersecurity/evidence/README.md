# Evidence Summary – B14

For this activity, I explained phishing scams to one of my friends.

I told them that phishing is when someone sends a fake email, text message, or link to trick people into giving away personal information, passwords, or bank details.

I explained a few warning signs, such as:
- messages that sound urgent
- links that look slightly wrong
- emails from strange addresses
- messages asking for passwords or payment details
- websites that look fake or badly written

I also told them that they should avoid clicking links from unknown senders and should check the sender address before trusting an email. I explained that using two-factor authentication can help protect an account even if a password gets stolen.

The main point I tried to teach was that phishing works by rushing people or making them panic, so it is better to slow down and check before clicking anything.
