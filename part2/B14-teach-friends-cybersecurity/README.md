# Activity B14 – Teaching Friends Cybersecurity

## Objective
To teach friends about a cybersecurity topic

## What I did
I explained phishing scams to one of my friends and talked about how they can avoid falling for them.

## Findings
I explained that phishing is when attackers send fake emails, text messages, or links to trick people into giving away personal information, passwords, or bank details.

I told them that phishing messages often try to rush the person or make the message sound urgent. This can make people click links without thinking properly.

I also explained some warning signs, such as strange sender email addresses, links that look wrong, messages asking for passwords, and websites that look fake or badly written.

We also talked about using two-factor authentication because it can help protect an account if a password gets stolen.

## Evidence collected
I included a short written summary of what I explained in the evidence folder.

## Reflection
This activity showed me that phishing is a simple topic to explain, but it is still very important. A lot of people know scams exist, but they do not always know what signs to look for.
