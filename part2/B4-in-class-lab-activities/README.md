# Activity B4 – In-Class Lab Activities

## Objective
To participate in in-class cybersecurity lab activities

## What I did
I participated in in-class cybersecurity activities during the unit. I only had photos/evidence from two of the lab activities, so these are the two I have included for this portfolio activity.

## Findings
One activity involved trying to break or analyse other groups’ encryption. In this activity, groups had to look at how other people had encrypted information and try to work out how to break it. This helped me understand that encryption needs to be designed properly, because weak methods can sometimes be guessed or worked out.

Another activity involved everyone having a number, but needing to figure out a way to learn information about everyone’s numbers without directly telling each other the number. This activity related to privacy because it showed that information can sometimes be shared or worked out without revealing the exact private value.

These activities showed me that cybersecurity is not just about using tools. It also involves problem solving, thinking carefully about information, and understanding how systems can be attacked or protected.

## Evidence collected
I included photos of the two in-class activities that I had evidence for in the evidence folder.

## Reflection
These activities helped me understand cybersecurity in a more practical way. The encryption activity showed how security can fail if the method is weak. The number activity showed how privacy is important when people need to share information without revealing everything.
