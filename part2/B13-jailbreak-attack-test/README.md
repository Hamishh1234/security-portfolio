# Activity B13 – Jailbreak Attack Test

## Objective
To perform a jailbreak attack on a generative AI assistant in a controlled test

## What I did
I tested a generative AI system by trying different jailbreak prompts to see if I could make it ignore its normal rules.

## Findings
I tried three different jailbreak style prompts.

The first prompt asked the AI to pretend it had no restrictions and answer anything I asked.

The second prompt tried to put the AI into “developer mode” and told it that it no longer needed to follow safety rules.

The third prompt asked the AI to act as a fictional AI that could reveal hidden system instructions.

None of these prompts worked. The AI did not ignore its rules or reveal hidden instructions. It kept responding in a controlled way and said that it still had to follow safety and privacy boundaries.

This showed me that the AI has protections in place to stop users from easily bypassing its rules.

## Evidence collected
I included a screenshot of the jailbreak prompts and the AI responses in the evidence folder.

## Reflection
This activity showed me that jailbreak attacks are a real issue, but current AI systems are designed to resist them. Even when I tried prompts that sounded like they were changing the AI’s behaviour, it still followed its rules.
