# Activity B15 – Teaching an Elderly Person Cybersecurity

## Objective
To teach an elderly person about a cybersecurity topic

## What I did
I explained scam text messages and phone call scams to an elderly person.

## Findings
I explained that scammers often pretend to be trusted organisations such as banks, delivery companies, government services, or phone companies.

I told them that scam messages often try to make the person panic or act quickly. For example, they might say an account will be locked, a delivery failed, or money needs to be paid straight away.

I also explained that they should not click links from unknown numbers, give out passwords, or share verification codes over the phone.

We also talked about checking with someone they trust before replying to suspicious messages or sending money.

## Evidence collected
I included a short written summary of what I explained in the evidence folder.

## Reflection
This activity showed me that cybersecurity advice needs to be explained in a simple way depending on the person. Scam messages can look real, so it is important to slow down and check before trusting them.
