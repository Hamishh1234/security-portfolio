# Evidence Summary – B15

For this activity, I explained scam messages and phone call scams to an elderly person.

I explained that scammers often send fake text messages or make phone calls pretending to be from banks, delivery companies, the government, or other trusted organisations.

I told them that one warning sign is when a message sounds urgent or says they need to act quickly. I also explained that they should not click links from unknown numbers or give out passwords, banking details, or verification codes over the phone.

I also explained that if they are unsure, it is better to stop and ask someone they trust before replying, clicking links, or sending money.

The main point I tried to teach was that scammers often try to pressure people, so slowing down and checking first can help avoid mistakes.
