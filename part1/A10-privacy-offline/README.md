# Activity A10 – Privacy Technique Offline

## Objective
To identify a privacy technique used offline

## What I did
I looked at everyday used devices and how they protect personal information when offline (not connected to the internet)

## Findings
One example of an offline privacy technique is using a passcode to lock a iphone.

If a iphone is locked it requires a password before being able to access anything. This prevent other people from viewing personal data such as messages, photos, and apps unless they know this password.

This is important because if a phone is lost or stolen the passcode adds protection and makes it harder for someone to access the information on the device.

## Evidence collected
Screenshot of a locked iPhone showing the passcode screen

## Reflection
Privacy isnt always to do with being online. Privacy can be small things (such as a password to access a iphone) that protect you from physical threats.
