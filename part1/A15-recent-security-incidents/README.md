# Activity A15 – Recent Security Incidents

## Objective
To look at recent cybersecurity incidents and understand what happened

## What I did
I researched online recent cybersecurity incidents and looked at different websites about different attacks/incedents and chose 5 of them.

## Findings

- Ticketmaster – "Ticketmaster is one of the world's largest ticket sales and distribution companies" (stated in the web page), which shows how large companies can still be vulnerable. The ticketmaster data breach was a cyberattack where customer details were leaked online and sold on dark web forums.

- Optus – "Optus is Austrlia's second-largest mobile carrier" (stated in the web page). This attack was a major data breach in Australia where personal information of customers was exposed due to poor security. The hacker had accessed between 2.5 - 9.7 million records. The leak included very important information such as driver licenses.

- Medibank – Hackers accessed sensitive health data and released it, which had serious privacy impacts. This put 9.7 million people at risk of exploitation and fraud. The hacker group was identified to be from Russia. They demanaded a ransom of US $1 per affected customer (totaling  A$15 million)

- Microsoft – A security breach involving email systems where attackers were able to access a very small percentage of Microsoft coporate email accounts. 

- Change Healthcare – A ransomware attack that disrupted healthcare services and systems. The hackers stole large amounts of data then deployed ransomeware to lock systems and shut down operations. This affected places such as hospitals as they could not process payments. The company paid $22 million in bitcoin to get their systems back and to try and stop the data from being leaked.
